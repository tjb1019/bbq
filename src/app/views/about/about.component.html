About

     The Sullivan’s barbeque heritage began many decades ago on a farm in North Carolina and has been going strong for three generations. With sixteen children, great granddaddy Sullivan had lots of mouths to feed and barbeque was a good way to do it! He passed that passion down to Pop (Billy) Sullivan who took that skill, perfected the recipe, and turned barbeque into an opportunity for family and friends to gather and celebrate. As a Ruritan Club member Pop started “cooking pigs” for fundraisers and to support the community. His kids (Robert is # 4 of 5) remembers as a young child helping Pop at the cooker at the club as people lined up get their share.

     That progressed to barbeque fundraisers at the Ebenezer Methodist Church (with his friend Robert Powell) – an annual event which became known far and wide. Pop helped organize the event and cooked over 300 pigs at a time to raise funds that would serve the community. And then, there were the family “pig pickins"... pretty much anyone was considered family. If you were able to come that day, knew how to say y’all or dawg (dog for anyone over the Mason Dixon line), were willing to help, and LOVED barbeque – you were welcome to become an honorary Sullivan.

     From this heritage Robert developed his passion for barbequing and a love for sharing his talent with others. Our family and friends all have their favorite – some love the NC style whole pig, some would jump off the Brooklyn Bridge to get some of his brisket, Darlene’s favorite is the chicken… and the ribs…. oh the ribs!

     Darlene married into all of this craziness and had a lot to learn. By definition she is a southern girl because she was born on the Sumter S.C. Air Force Base and she did live in Mississippi for a while but in reality she was one of those darn Yankees having been raised primarily in Wilmington, DE and Chadds Ford, PA. And while her family weren’t barbeque masters, they were great barbeque lovers – always seeking out barbeque joints on our many travels. The most important things for Darlene to learn were how to cook the hushpuppies and that famous banana pudding that Granny Sullivan has perfected to a science.

     Many people have asked about the creation of our Sullivan’s Heritage Barbeque logo. To begin, the Sullivan family coat of arms shows a pig that is half black and half white. Obviously that was going to have to be included. Robert is a rabid North Carolina State University fan. The “Wolfpack” colors are red, black and white – which worked well into the picnic blanket theme for a barbeque. And thus, the logo was born. We look forward to meeting you and will be happy to make you an honorary Sullivan!
